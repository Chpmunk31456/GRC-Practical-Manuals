# GRC Practical Manuals

## Governance, Risk, Compliance, Resilience, and Security

A free, practical, and beginner-friendly collection of Governance, Risk, and Compliance manuals for students, junior analysts, managers, cybersecurity professionals, auditors, consultants, nonprofit organizations, and teams building or improving security and compliance programs.

**Author:** Alberto (Al) Leiva  
**Repository Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** July 2026

---

## About This Repository

The GRC Practical Manuals collection provides clear and practical guidance for understanding, implementing, documenting, testing, and improving cybersecurity governance, risk, compliance, privacy, assurance, cloud security, third-party risk, and operational resilience programs.

These manuals are designed to help readers move beyond theory and understand how GRC work is performed in real organizations.

The collection includes:

- Plain-language explanations
- Practical implementation guidance
- Framework and regulatory concepts
- Control examples
- Evidence-collection guidance
- Audit-readiness practices
- Risk-assessment methods
- Policy and procedure guidance
- Open-source tools and resources
- Checklists and templates
- Guidance for junior professionals
- Guidance for managers and program leaders

---

# Quick Start

1. Select the framework, regulation, or risk area relevant to your needs.
2. Open the corresponding numbered folder.
3. Open the manual-specific folder.
4. Review the folder `README.md`.
5. Download either the Microsoft Word or PDF edition.
6. Adapt the guidance to your organization’s risks, systems, contracts, and obligations.
7. Verify requirements through current official sources.
8. Document implementation and retain appropriate evidence.
9. Test controls regularly.
10. Track gaps, corrective actions, and continuous improvement.

Educational Mission

I want people to understand why this work matters so deeply to me.

I was born in 1956. As a Latino man with learning disabilities, I could easily have been underestimated, overlooked, or denied opportunities. I was fortunate to have mentors throughout my life who recognized my potential, encouraged me, and helped me find paths that I might never have discovered on my own.

Their guidance made it possible for me to build a career in technology, education, counseling, rehabilitation engineering, cybersecurity, and service to others. I have never forgotten what they did for me.

That is why I am committed to creating free manuals, career roadmaps, internship and fellowship directories, accessibility resources, and practical technology education.

Many people have the ability to make a meaningful difference in the world, but they lack money, guidance, accommodations, connections, or a clear path forward.

These resources are my way of helping make that path easier to find.

Responsible and Ethical Use

The manuals and resources in this repository must be used responsibly, ethically, and legally.

Cybersecurity examples should only be applied to:

Systems you own
Systems you are authorized to manage
Systems for which you have written permission
Approved training or laboratory environments

Technical ability does not create permission.

Do not use these materials to:

Access systems without authorization
Circumvent security controls
Violate privacy
Steal credentials
Deploy malicious software
Cause operational disruption
Conduct deceptive or harmful activity
