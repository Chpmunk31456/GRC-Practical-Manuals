 GRC — Governance, Risk, and Compliance

A practical, beginner-friendly collection of Governance, Risk, and Compliance manuals for managers, junior analysts, cybersecurity professionals, students, and organizations building or improving a security and compliance program.

**Author:** Alberto (Al) Leiva

---

## About This Repository

This repository provides practical guidance for understanding, implementing, documenting, and maintaining cybersecurity governance, risk, compliance, privacy, resilience, and assurance programs.

The manuals are written to help readers move beyond theory. They include implementation guidance, examples, evidence-collection ideas, audit-readiness considerations, control-mapping concepts, and references to useful tools.

The material is intended for:

- Junior GRC and cybersecurity analysts
- Security and compliance managers
- IT and security professionals moving into GRC
- Students and career changers
- Small and medium-sized organizations
- Consultants, assessors, and program owners
- Anyone who wants a clearer understanding of security and compliance frameworks

---

## Manuals Included

The repository currently includes practical manuals covering:

| Topic | Primary Focus |
|---|---|
| **CIS Critical Security Controls v8.1** | Prioritized safeguards for reducing common cybersecurity risks |
| **Cloud Security and Cloud Compliance** | Governance, controls, evidence, and risk management for cloud environments |
| **GDPR** | Privacy principles, data-subject rights, accountability, and compliance practices |
| **HIPAA** | Administrative, physical, and technical safeguards for protected health information |
| **ISO/IEC 27001 and ISO/IEC 27002** | Information security management systems and supporting security controls |
| **Incident Response, Business Continuity, and Disaster Recovery** | Preparation, response, recovery, resilience, testing, and documentation |
| **NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0** | Cybersecurity risk management using Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover |
| **NIST Risk Management Framework and SP 800-53 Release 5.2.0** | System-level risk management, control selection, assessment, authorization, and monitoring |
| **PCI DSS v4.0.1** | Protection of payment-card environments and cardholder data |
| **SOC 2** | Trust Services Criteria, control design, evidence, readiness, and assurance |
| **Third-Party Risk Management and Cyber Supply Chain Security** | Vendor due diligence, monitoring, contractual safeguards, and supply-chain risk |

Most subjects are provided in both **PDF** and **Microsoft Word** formats when available.

## What You Will Learn

Depending on the manual, readers will learn how to:

- Understand the purpose and scope of major security and privacy frameworks
- Translate requirements into practical policies, procedures, and controls
- Identify control owners and define responsibilities
- Perform risk assessments and document treatment decisions
- Collect and organize evidence for audits and assessments
- Build registers, matrices, inventories, and compliance trackers
- Evaluate control design and operating effectiveness
- Prepare for internal reviews and external audits
- Use open-source and commercial tools responsibly
- Maintain a sustainable continuous-compliance program
- Communicate risk clearly to technical teams, managers, and executives

## Suggested Learning Path

Readers who are new to GRC may find this order helpful:

1. **NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0**
2. **CIS Critical Security Controls v8.1**
3. **ISO/IEC 27001 and ISO/IEC 27002**
4. **SOC 2**
5. **Incident Response, Business Continuity, and Disaster Recovery**
6. **Third-Party Risk Management and Cyber Supply Chain Security**
7. **Cloud Security and Cloud Compliance**
8. **NIST RMF and SP 800-53**
9. **PCI DSS, HIPAA, or GDPR**, depending on industry and regulatory needs

This is only a suggested path. Readers may begin with the framework most relevant to their organization or career goals.

## How to Use the Manuals

1. Select the manual that matches your goal.
2. Use the PDF version for reading and reference.
3. Use the Word version for notes, study, internal adaptation, or controlled customization.
4. Review the scope, terminology, roles, implementation steps, and evidence examples.
5. Compare the guidance with the official standard, regulation, or authoritative publication.
6. Adapt templates and examples to the organization’s actual environment.
7. Record approvals, exceptions, risks, testing results, and corrective actions.
8. Review the program regularly and update it when systems, risks, laws, or standards change.

## Important Notice

These manuals are educational resources. They do not replace:

- Official standards, laws, regulations, or contractual requirements
- Legal advice
- Regulatory interpretation
- Qualified audit, certification, or assessment services
- Organization-specific risk analysis
- Guidance from an authorized assessor, attorney, privacy professional, or regulator

Requirements can change. Always verify critical decisions against current official sources and the specific obligations that apply to your organization, industry, location, systems, and contracts.

A document alone does not establish compliance. Effective compliance requires approved governance, implemented controls, trained personnel, reliable evidence, testing, corrective action, and continuing oversight.
